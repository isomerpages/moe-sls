<h1>My Notebooks (New)</h1>
<h2>About My Notebooks</h2>
<p>Students can create their own notebooks for note-taking and add notes
from assignments, consolidating everything in one place. They can also
organise their notebooks into categories, making it easy to locate and
build upon their notes, which helps make revision more effective.</p>
<h3>Create a Notebook</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Open the left menu and select <strong>My Notebooks</strong>. To pin it
to the menu, click <strong>Edit Shortcuts</strong>, select <strong>My Notebooks</strong> under <strong>Shortcuts available</strong>,
and click <strong>Save</strong>.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Create Notebook</strong> to start a new notebook. You can
also search by notebook title and filter by category in the notebook listing.
For each notebook, click the overflow (<strong>3-dot</strong>) icon to
move it to another category, uncategorise, or delete it.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Your notebooks will appear in List view by default. You can choose to
view your notebooks in Card view by clicking on the note card icon.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Create Category</strong> to organise your notebooks. For each
category, click the overflow (<strong>3-dot</strong>) icon to move, rename,
or delete it.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Create_Notebook_1.png">
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>After creating a new notebook, enter the title, upload an image as cover,
and add a description. Click anywhere outside the component to auto-save.
To make changes later, click the pencil icon.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Create_Notebook_2.png">
</div>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Note: After organising your notes, you can create a contents page on the
notebook cover by inserting links to other notebook pages in the description
section.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/image__6_.png">
</div>
<ol start="3" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Expand the drawer and click <strong>Add New Page</strong> to insert pages
into the notebook. Navigate between pages using the bottom navigation bar.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Click the <strong>pencil icon</strong> to edit the page title, or use the
overflow (<strong>3-dot</strong>) icon to add/move a page, add/remove colour
tab, or delete the page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>For the colour tab, you can choose from three colours. Use them to organise
your pages or spotlight certain pages—for example:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Blue: Key concepts, exam topics</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Orange: Revision or review required</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Pink: Connection between chapters, reflections</p>
</li>
</ul>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Create_Notebook_3.png">
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="4" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Add Note</strong> to add content to each page. Click the <strong>3-dot</strong> icon
to expand or collapse the Rich Text Editor and use its tools to insert
content. Learn more about the Rich Text Editor <a href="https://www.learning.moe.edu.sg/teacher-user-guide/author/about-rich-text-editor/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Create_Notebook_4.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>To view and print all pages in a continuous format, open the right menu
and select <strong>View as Print-Friendly Notebook</strong>.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Create_Notebook_5.png">
</div>
<p><strong>Note: </strong>You can also adjust the appearance settings: wide
or standard view.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p></p>
<h3>Add Notes from <strong>Assignments</strong> or <strong>Self-Study modules</strong> into a <strong>Notebook</strong></h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>In an assignment or self-study module, click the <strong>Magnifying </strong>Glass <strong>icon </strong>to
view the notes.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_1.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>To add the notes to a notebook, click <strong>Add to Notebook</strong>.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_2.png">
</div>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="3" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Select the notebook where you want to add the notes. If the notes have
already been added to a notebook, it will appear under <strong>“Added to”.</strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_3.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Select the page to add the notes to, then click <strong>Add</strong> to
confirm.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_4.png">
</div>
</li>
</ol>
<ol start="5" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>A temporary message will appear when the notes have been successfully
added to the page. You can click on the ‘View’ to open the notebook in
a new tab.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_5.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Alternatively, you can open the left menu and select <strong>My Notebooks.</strong> Go
to the notebook to view the newly added notes. Content in the notebook:</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Source showing the title of the assignment/self-study module (as a URL
linking back to the module)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Annotated text or media object (if any)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Self-created notes</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="7" data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Ways to manage or organise the notes:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Click the overflow (<strong>3-dot</strong>) icon to move the notes, move
them to a specific page within the same notebook, or delete them.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>When moving the notes to a specific page, select the page from the dropdown
list and click <strong>Save</strong> to confirm.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click the pencil icon to edit the notes.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Add_notes_to_Notebook_6.png">
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Note:</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>When notes are edited in either <strong>My Notebooks</strong> or <strong>Assignment/Self-study module</strong>,
the changes will be reflected in both.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>New notes will always be added below the existing notes.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p></p>
<h3>Unavailable Notes or Annotations</h3>
<p>A placeholder will be displayed when notes or annotations become unavailable
because you have lost access. This could be due to some of the following
reasons:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The notes or annotations in the assignment or resource have been deleted
by the owner.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The assignment or resource has been deleted or unpublished.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The assignment or resource has been paused by the teacher.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/image001__1_.png">
</div>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Note: </strong>You should check again before removing such notes
or annotations from your notebook.</p>
