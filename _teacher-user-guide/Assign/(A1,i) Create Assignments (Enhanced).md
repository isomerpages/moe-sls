<h1>(A1,i) Create Assignments (Enhanced)</h1>
<hr>
<p>You can create an assignment by assigning a module to students in your
Class Groups.</p>
<p>You can turn your assignment into an <a href="/teacher-user-guide/assess/set-assignments-as-assessments/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">assessment</a>.
An Assessment Card is differentiated with a beige background.</p>
<p><u>Note</u>: For a better user experience, assignments with over 100 assignees
will be split into multiple assignments with a maximum of 100 students.</p>
<h2>Assign a Module</h2>
<hr>
<p><strong>Important</strong>: If module permissions to allow teachers to
copy/assign/publish and students to copy this module are not checked, it
will not be possible to assign the module.</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>From the homepage, open the left menu and click <strong>Resources</strong>,
followed by <strong>My Drive</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Open</strong> under the Module Title.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>On the Module View page, click the <strong>Assign</strong> icon found on
the top right of the header.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width:1.5rem; display: inline;" height="auto" width="100%" src="/images/Icons/Assign.svg">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>On the Assign Module subpage, you will see all the Class Groups that are
assigned to you. Class Groups that you have created can be found here too.
Select the Class Groups you would like to assign to, then click <strong>Assign</strong>.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 80%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Create Assignments" src="/images/2Teacher/AS_CreateAssignment3.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Alternatively, from a <a href="/teacher-user-guide/organise/about-class-groups/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Class Group</a>,
under the Assignments tab, click <strong>Add Assignment</strong> to assign
a module.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>On the Assignment Title subpage, you may edit the <strong>Assignment Title</strong> and <strong>Permission Settings</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>You may also pause the assignment upon assigning by checking the relevant
checkbox.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 80%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Create Assignments" src="/images/2Teacher/AS_CreateAssignment4.png">
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>You may also adjust the permission settings. These permission settings
will not affect your current module in My Drive, but subsequent copies
of this assignment.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Next</strong> to proceed to the Assignment Summary Page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click&nbsp;<strong>Assign</strong>, found on the top right of the <a href="/teacher-user-guide/assign/manage-assignments/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Assignment Summary page</a> to
assign the module.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Note: For modules with multiple sections, it may take some time for the
assignment to appear on your assignment list.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click on the right menu and select <strong>Access via QR code</strong> to
generate a shortened URL and QR Code with SLS branding for students to
access the assignment. After this, the Student Attempt URL will automatically
change to the shortened URL.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 80%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Create Assignments" src="/images/2Teacher/ASS_qrcode_4.png">
</div>
</li>
</ol>

<h2>How to Assign Linked Assignments</h2>
<hr>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>You can assign linked assignments within and across multiple Student Groups
and Subgroups or Form Classes at the Assignment Summary Page, by selecting
the <strong>Link multiple assignments</strong> checkbox.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/2Teacher/AS_CreateAssignment5.png">
</div>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><u>Note</u>: When editing the assigned module, all linked assignments
will be paused, and changes made will be applied to all linked assignments.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
