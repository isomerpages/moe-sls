<h1>(E1,iv) About Adaptive Learning System (ALS)</h1>
<hr>
<p>Adaptive Learning System (ALS) provides a customised learning pathway
for each student, recommending learning resources and practice questions
and offering immediate feedback based on his/her readiness. Students can
access ALS through the Student Learning Space (SLS) for self-directed learning.</p>
<p>In MOE Library, teachers and students can also find content tagged to
ALS content maps if the content is set to be visible to them.</p>
<p>With ALS, students learn independently, at their own pace, according to
their strengths and needs.</p>
<p>Teachers who wish to experience ALS can open the right menu and click&nbsp;<strong>Switch to Student Account and click Resources, followed by Adaptive Learning.</strong>
</p>

<ol>
<li>
<p><u>Note</u>: Access to ALS for students is dependent on their current
level of study. Students can access ALS content below their current level
of learning and one level above their current level of learning. (If a
student is in Primary 5, they will be able to access content tagged to
Primary 6, together with content from Primary 1 to 4).
<br>Access to ALS for teachers is dependent on the highest level that the
teacher is teaching, e.g. if a teacher is teaching up to Primary 5, then
the access to ALS would be up to Primary 6 (one level above highest teaching
level) and all levels below (i.e. Primary 1 to4). If a teacher wishes to
access ALS content beyond this, they &nbsp;can add themselves to a Class
Group, tagged to the appropriate levels, as a student following the steps
in <a href="/teacher-user-guide/organise/add-teachers-as-students-to-a-class-group/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Add Teachers as Students to a Class Group</a>
</p>
